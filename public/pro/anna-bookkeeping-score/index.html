Bookkeeping Score for ANNA Money

I designed a tool that makes a boring task enjoyable, and I boosted subscriptions for the company.

My role was to:

Year

• Conduct product design
• Support creative design
• Inspire stakeholders

2023

Who is ANNA?

ANNA Money offers two solutions to small business owners: bank accounts and digital tools for business administration.

The challenge

The aim was to create engagement in an area that is notoriously challenging: bookkeeping. Instead of having people avoid it, I sought to change this behaviour by making the process enjoyable.

Our metrics

We would look to improve:

  1. The proportion of tidy books in ANNA.
  2. Conversion from trial to paid on the premium subscription for tax tools.

Who was on board?

  • 1 Product Design Lead – Me
  • 1 Creative Designer
  • 2 Native App Developers

My contribution

I conducted experience design and led this project end-to-end. I worked with a visual designer who was managing the component library, to ensure efficient delivery of final assets.

Step 1

Targeting the core issue

Customers were subscribed to ANNA's tax filing solution, yet their books were noticeably unkempt. Internally, we were struggling to file tax returns in an efficient way with their books being messy.

Problem statement

How might we foster engagement in the difficult and dreaded task of bookkeeping? Commonly experienced as monotonous, we need to shift this attitude by making the bookkeeping process enjoyable and captivating.

Step 2

Exploring and validating the idea

I quickly explored a rough version of the idea with people in our customer feedback panel, and worked with data science to develop a useful model.

Testing a prototype

I created an interactive prototype using Figma, wrote testing questions, and organised participants to meet online.

I recorded the testing sessions via Zoom and summarised the responses to share within our product team.

Early on, we explored a desktop solution, but switched to mobile due to the better volume of users.

Results from user testing

Some things I observed when testing a prototype of this feature:

  • Users loved the score because it provided clarity.
  • The type of tasks we suggested matched the users’ mental model.
  • The user interface for changing tax categories needed to be more clear.

Creating a model with the Data Science team

I shared the vision with Data Science and we collaborated to create a system for a great experience, with only the most important accounting issues being highlighted.

We established how we might build on ANNA’s proprietary AI system and ask for user input only when a task completed automatically contained a low confidence rating.

Step 3

Wireframing detailed solutions

I created wireframes to quickly gather feedback and to ensure all details were available to design final assets. I created wireframes to quickly gather feedback and to ensure all details were available to design final assets.

Chat skills

ANNA uses an assistance-style interaction model to communicate about features with it’s customer base.

I write conversation skills that were brief, to the point, useful and friendly. With information broken down into digestible chunks, users were found happy to engage.

There were a number of scenarios I created flows for, depending on how users interacted with tasks.

Score overview and completing tasks

I fostered delight with customers by creating simple layouts that brought key information to the fore.

The most important aspect was the score, which would update once the user confirmed accounting categories and attached receipts.

Step 4

Maintaining buy-in

I presented the concept with strong rationales, to make sure those in charge stayed onboard with the potential value of the project.

A chat to keep the team aligned

There was a point when executive leaders and engineering folk questioned the project. I booked a chat to go over the benefits to the user and how it solved a business problem. It was a great result to become realigned with full support after talking about the vision.

Final solutions

I worked with a visual designer on things like colours, UI buttons, and good wording to use. Together we made it a more polished solution.

Chat-based interactions that engage

ANNA uses conversation design for many of it’s features. I designed flows and wrote copy for the Bookkeeping Score chat skills, including feature awareness, weekly reminders and end-of-trial subscription prompts. These outperformed other similar chat skills in existence, I believe due to their intriguing nature.

Tinder-style task completion

I leveraged the ‘swipe right’ UX pattern found in many digital tools, making it simple and pleasant to complete tasks. It was a great way to boil down complex information and provide focus for the user. The pattern reduces cognitive load.

Reward mechanism

I made sure users feel rewarded for their bookkeeping work. They reach a high score, and feel happy that their work is done.


Customers told me in one-on-one user research that they want to save money on taxes, but they don’t know how to. I included a ‘potential tax saving’ that highlights how much they can save if they complete their tasks – by firming up the accounting categories on their expenses.

Monitoring performance

Just after release, I checked how the release was going.

Mapping results to the flow

I created user flows to outline key pages for development. As a quick post-release check, I gathered performance results from the development team and added them to the flow. This highlighted areas where it outperformed other initiatives and also gave us a guide for future improvements.

The impact

Activation of the feature and completion of bookkeeping tasks is high, and there are a valuable number of users upgrading to a paid plan to keep using the feature after their trial period.

Users tidying books

A large number of bookkeeping tasks are being completed, enabling us to meet our objective of preparing tax returns.

Conversion to paid

This feature performs really well at converting trialists of our tax filing plan to paid customers.
